Overview

Prototypes are a great way to test out new ideas and demonstrate how a product will work before getting deep into development.

You will be researching similar products and creating a prototype of your app for Project and Portfolio IV. This is done in order to have most of the UI design done before jumping into development, and also to get valuable feedback from your course director and classmates, in case something needs to be re-worked or re-factored.

Preparation

Research products similar to our project. Note how those products have made smart UX decisions. Note features that are missing from these similar products that we believe would make for a better UX in our project/product.

Create a GUI feature list. This list will include features we are borrowing/copying from the similar products researched in our pitches, and original features that we noted are missing from those similar products. Give details on each of the features, what needs will they meet for our user. Think back to the greenlighted pitch, the similar products mentioned in it, apply that level of detail to our features.

Create (or gather) the necessary assets to put together your prototype.

Pick a tool to build the interface of the product/project. There are a number of tools you can use to help put your prototype together.

The Prototype

Take what has been learned in the research preparation done above of other products/apps/websites, and create a rapid prototype using your prototyping tool. This prototype needs to include every single interaction in the list of features. Focus first on the primary interactions the user will make with the application, as well as the basic flow of the application. After the primary interactions are in place, implement all remaining features/interactions the app with provide. Graphics and iconography do not need to be finalized, but should be a somewhat fair indication of how the final app will appear.

All projects are different, so the amount of interactions/features the prototype will support is variable.

Submissions

A (.pdf) document that notes which similar products were researched, what parts our interface will borrow from those products, and where our product will improve upon poor interface choices.
Also list the prioritized features the product/project will have, and the ones the prototype can accomplish currently.
